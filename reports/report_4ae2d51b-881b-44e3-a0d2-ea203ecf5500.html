Executive Summary

This new Power BI dashboard provides a critical capability for proactive supplier risk management across all business units. Its primary objective is to offer immediate and comprehensive visibility into the supplier landscape, enabling business users to swiftly identify high-risk suppliers and implement corrective actions. By centralizing key performance indicators such as Total Suppliers, High/Medium/Low Risk classifications, Average Supplier Risk Score, On-Time Delivery Percentage, Quality Rating, and Purchase Spend, the dashboard significantly enhances our ability to prevent supply disruptions, thereby safeguarding operational continuity and maintaining business resilience. The dashboard's design allows for deep, actionable insights by breaking down these metrics across critical dimensions including Business Unit, individual Supplier Name, and Time. This granular view empowers users to not only identify *which* suppliers pose the greatest risk but also to understand the context of that risk in relation to their delivery performance, quality standards, and financial impact. Ultimately, this tool facilitates data-driven decision-making for strategic sourcing, targeted supplier development programs, and more effective risk mitigation strategies, ensuring a robust and reliable supply chain.

Business Objective

To build an executive Power BI dashboard to monitor supplier risk across all business units and enable business users to identify high-risk suppliers and take corrective action to prevent supply disruptions.

Recommendations

Here are 3-5 specific recommendations for implementation based on the provided BI analysis: 1. **Standardize Risk Score Methodology & Thresholds:** Before building visualizations, clearly define the calculation methodology for the "Average Supplier Risk Score" and establish precise, data-driven thresholds for classifying suppliers as "High Risk," "Medium Risk," and "Low Risk." This ensures consistency, objectivity, and that all users understand *why* a supplier falls into a specific category, enabling more effective and standardized corrective actions. 2. **Implement Granular Drill-Through for Root Cause Analysis:** Develop interactive drill-through capabilities from aggregate risk views (e.g., total high-risk suppliers) down to individual supplier profiles. These profiles should detail the contributing factors to their risk score, showcasing historical trends for "On-Time Delivery Percentage," specific "Quality Rating" issues, and "Purchase Spend" impact. This empowers business users to identify the specific reasons for high risk and formulate targeted corrective actions. 3. **Establish Proactive Alerting Mechanisms:** Configure automated alerts within Power BI or integrated systems to notify relevant stakeholders (e.g., procurement managers, supply chain leads) when a supplier's risk score crosses a predefined high-risk threshold, when key performance metrics significantly deteriorate, or when a new supplier is classified as high-risk. This proactive notification system is crucial for "preventing supply disruptions" by enabling swift intervention. 4. **Integrate Corrective Action Tracking & Feedback Loop:** Beyond identifying risk, the dashboard should facilitate action. Implement (or plan for integration with) a system or module that allows users to log, track, and monitor the progress of corrective actions initiated for high-risk suppliers directly from the dashboard or a linked application. Additionally, establish a feedback mechanism for users to suggest improvements to the dashboard, ensuring it continuously meets evolving business needs.
